My Story

About Me

For sixteen years, I have had the sacred privilege of working with the human body — its resilience, its quiet intelligence, its capacity for transformation. I believe that touch, offered with full presence and genuine care, is among the most ancient and healing gifts we can offer one another. My work is not simply technique applied to muscle; it is a form of listening. A living conversation between intention and release, between what the body has been holding and what it is finally, quietly, ready to let go of.

My path began with Swedish massage — that foundational language of long, flowing strokes that teaches us how breath and circulation move as one, how the nervous system learns to soften and trust. From there, the river carried me wider: into the dosha wisdom of Ayurveda, warm herbal oils moving along ancient marma pathways; into the mat-based poetry of Thai massage, with its acupressure and assisted opening; into the oceanic spirit of Lomi Lomi, where continuous wave-like strokes reach something deep and cellular. I have received Reiki, studied forearm deep tissue work, reflexology, and the profound medicine of sound. Each tradition I have carried not as a credential to accumulate, but as a doorway into the vast intelligence of the body.

Some of my most alive moments as a therapist have happened not in a treatment room, but at a gathering. On the floor of an ecstatic dance event after the last song fades. At a holistic retreat where people arrive thin and scattered and leave feeling whole. At a conscious festival where strangers lie down on my table and rise quietly changed. I am drawn to the edges of the ordinary — to those spaces where community gathers to remember something essential about living fully in a body. This is where I find my deepest work, and where I come home to my truest self.
